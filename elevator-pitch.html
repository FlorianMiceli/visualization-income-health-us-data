INCA3 — social inequalities & diet

What this project is

An interactive dashboard built on the French national nutrition survey (INCA3): weighted patterns of food consumption, nutrient intakes, food groups, and simple composite diet-quality views — broken down by social strata (for example income).

Observable Framework · D3 · survey weights (descriptive, not causal).

Who it is for

Audience

  • Public health & policy — quick, evidence-aligned views of inequality in what people eat.
  • Researchers & students — reproducible charts instead of only raw tables.
  • Anyone exploring how diet-related indicators differ across groups in a national sample.

Why it matters

Interest & importance

Diet is a lever for population health, and social gradients in food access and quality are a core equity issue. Official microdata are rich but heavy to navigate; this project makes key contrasts visible, comparable, and tied to transparent visualization choices.

It supports informed discussion — not replacement of causal studies or official reporting.
